**Opening**

Good Afternoon everyone. Our team has conducted a thorough analysis of Electrolux’s competitive position in the global major appliances market, and I’m here to walk you through our key findings on where your strongest competitive advantages lie and how we think you should be thinking about them going forward.

To structure this analysis, we’ve applied two complementary frameworks, the first being Porter’s Generic Competitive Advantage model, which identifies whether a company competes through cost leadership, differentiation or focus. The second is the VRIN framework, which examines whether an advantage is Valuable, Rare, Inimitable and Non-substitutable. Together we believe these two frameworks not only identify what your advantages are, but help us assess how durable they are against an increasingly aggressive competitive landscape.

***Porter’s Framework***

**Moving to Porter’s Framework**

Porter tells us that competitive advantage comes from one of three strategic positions, the first being cost leadership, where you win on efficiency and price, the second being differentiation, where you  win on unique features, design or quality and the third being focus, where you concentrate on a specific niche. What makes Electrolux distinctive is that you have successfully built a dual-track strategy, operating across both cost leadership and differentiation simultaneously.

On the cost leadership side, the evidence is clear. As of 2016, approximately 60% of production takes place in low-cost areas located near growing markets, and the business has delivered 4 billion SEK in cost reductions in both 2024 and 2025, a total of 12,8 billion SEK since 2023.

On the differentiation side, AEG has earned seven independent StiWa awards in the laundry category within 24 months, an industry-leading achievement in Europe’s most demanding consumer testing environment. The Electrolux brand, built on over a century of Scandinavian design heritage, is positioned firmly in the premium segment.

The brand architecture behind this dual-track strategy has itself been a deliberate decision consolidating from six strategic brands down to three, each with a clear role: Electrolux for global premium, AEG for European precision and Frigidaire for the North American mass market.

***VRIN Analysis***

**Moving to the VRIN analysis**

By using the VRIN lens, we’ve identified four assets that form the backbone of your competitive position

**1. Global Mult-Brand Portfolio**

First, your brand portfolio. While competitors tend to dominate in one region or one segment, you have built and sustained genuine multi-regional market leadership across all four regions simultaneously, something that is truly rare in this industry. You hold approximately 31% market share in Brazil, your largest Latin American market, 40% in Australia and 16% in Western Europe. There is no competitor that can claim that kind of breadth. This took a very long time with a large amount of resources to build, and is a rare and inimitable advantage

**2. Consumer Insight & The 360-Degree Experience Model**

Second, your consumer insight culture. This goes back to the company's founding DNA, Wenner-Gren built this business on door-to-door sales specifically to stay close to the consumer, and that philosophy never left. Today it manifests in the 360-Degree Consumer Experience Model that allow you to engage customers from pre-purchase inspiration through to post-purchase evaluation. Your 2025 strategy makes this even more explicit, when choosing the new company vision to be centered as being the industry leader in consumer satisfaction. This is an advantage that is embedded in how this company thinks and operates. A competitor cannot acquire that consumer empathy overnight.

**3. Manufacturing Scale & Cost Excellence**

Third, your manufacturing and cost structure. With 53 facilities across 18 countries and approximately 60% of production in low-cost areas near growing markets, you have built serious operational efficiency into the foundations of this business. But what makes this advantage durable is not the scale alone, it is how the cost discipline is structured. The Cost Excellence Program, your company-wide cost management initiative, does not sit in one department, it is embedded across R&D, procurement, manufacturing and supply chain. There is also Supplier partnerships that drive both cost efficiency and product innovation, and the program continues to evolve toward more sustainable long-term drivers like value engineering and best-cost country sourcing. This is no longer just cost-cutting, it is systematised operational discipline built to last.

**4. Professional B2B Credibility as a Premium Signal**

Fourth, and this one we’d encourage you to leverage more aggressively in your marketing, your professional appliance heritage. Electrolux’s professional division built a legacy where over a third of Michelin-Starred restaurants in Europe used Electrolux kitchen equipment. Although that division has since been spun off, the heritage does not disappear overnight. And critically, that professional knowledge does not stay in the restaurant, it flows directly into your consumer products. A recent example is Frigidaire’s Pizza Mode, the first and only residential oven capable of reaching temperatures previously only achievable in professional restaurant kitchens. That is professional heritage turned into a tangible consumer product innovation that competitors simply cannot match with the same credibility. It is a non-substitutable point of differentiation, and in our view it is currently one of your most underutilised assets.

***Strategic Vulnerabilities*** 

**And Finally**

There are also three vulnerabilities we want to flag directly.

First is China. China is the world’s largest appliance market by volume, and your current share is approximately 1%. It is a fiercely competitive market dominated by local players on very aggressive pricing, and that context matters, but it does not make the gap less significant.

Second is margin pressure in North America. It accounts for 34% of group net sales, making it your single largest market by revenue, yet the region is still operating at a loss. Significant revenue exposure without profitability is not a sustainable position

Third is that brands like Haier and Samsung are no longer just competing on price. They are actively pushing into the premium segment, which is exactly where Electrolux and AEG make their strongest case to consumers. This is perhaps the most important threat to monitor because it strikes at one of the core aspects that make you distinctive. The window to strengthen your premium position is now, while you still hold an advantage.

***Closing Recommendation*** 

In summary, Electrolux enters this next phase with a generally strong hand. Your brand architecture, consumer insight capability, manufacturing efficiency and professional credibility are all real durable advantages. Our recommendation is to double down on these rather than spread resources too thinly, trying to chase volume in markets where you lack a structural position. To more understand where that is, its important to understand customer behavior and this is what Oscar will now speak about.
